Kalimba

Kalimba

I wish these 43 seconds never ended. I dissolved immediately when I heard those resonant and marvel overtones in the YT video "Can't Help Falling In Love on Kalimba". So I started discovering the unique world of metal tines that vibrate underneath my fingers. Tutorial by tutorial, tabs by tabs - I started mastering this wildly spirited instrument.

The Kalimba differ from other instruments - the zigzag order of notes, limited range, thumb-only playing - but that sort of limiting only pushes me to discover new bizarre ways to express myself. I implement that kind of inductive approach not only in music but also while solving problems that include a highly limited yet exhausting amount of information.

Now, I run out to the wild field next to my house again and lie down on the prairie grass, looking at the highways far away on the horizon. I feel the ringing sound of this instrument resonates with a lone abandoned water tower behind me. The slow warm autumn wind waves my hair. The sunset reflects from the metal tines, making my eyes happily squint.

I repeat this ritual every week - it's the best way for me to distract from the routine and meditate. I shortly pause the high pace of my life, clear my lungs of worries and anxieties, and now I breathe fully again. That helped me a lot during exams and olympiads, where I had to be concentrated and not let fears perplex me.
