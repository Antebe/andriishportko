Theremin

The theremin is an electronic musical instrument controlled without physical contact. The only thing that can shift the pitch of the outcoming sound is the distance from the hands to the antenna.

That galvanizing concept evoked me to play on this instrument. However, not only it was unaffordable to purchase it, but also there was no such device in my area. Then, a striking idea flashed across my mind - to build theremin by myself.

Originally, the hand of the performer acts as the grounded plate of a variable capacitor: the position of the hand changes capacity. However, I decided to implement another conception: the pitch varies according to the amount of light that reaches the surface of the photoresistor. Therefore, the position of the hand between the photoresistor and the source of light controls the tone.

Eventually, I have completed the circuit on my breadboard with the assistance of the Internet. The project idea suited well to the title of our physics lesson about oscillations, so I decided to present my version of the theremin. The presentation was a success: every person in the class was engaged.
